# Content Analysis Prompt

Use this prompt when you have source content (blog post, document, brief) to analyze before creating a presentation.

---

## Prompt

```
Analyze this content for presentation transformation:

[PASTE CONTENT OR URL HERE]

---

Provide a structured analysis:

## 1. Core Thesis
What's the ONE thing the audience should remember? (1 sentence)

## 2. Key Points
List 5-7 major points with supporting evidence:
- Point: [claim]
  Evidence: [data, example, or proof]

## 3. Quotable Lines
Identify 3-5 memorable phrases that could become headlines:
- "..."

## 4. Data Points
List any statistics, metrics, or comparisons:
- [metric]: [value]

## 5. Narrative Arc
Identify the structure:
- Problem/Hook: [what grabs attention]
- Stakes: [why it matters]
- Solution: [the answer]
- Proof: [why to believe it]
- Action: [what to do next]

## 6. Gaps & Weaknesses
What's missing or could be challenged?
- [gap/weakness]

## 7. Target Audience
Who is this for and what do they care about?
- Primary: [audience]
- Key concerns: [what keeps them up at night]

## 8. Recommended Slide Count
Based on content depth: [X-Y slides]
```

---

## Expected Output

A structured brief that informs all subsequent phases:
- Clear thesis to anchor every slide
- Key points that become individual slides
- Quotable lines that become headlines
- Data that adds credibility
- Gaps to address proactively
- Audience context for tone/depth decisions
